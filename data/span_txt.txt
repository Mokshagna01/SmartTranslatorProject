_________________________________ _________________ _ _________________ _ ___________ Introduction to the drafting of a technical report What is a technical report?

A technical report is a formal document that presents information about a technical topic, project, experiment or process in a systematic, factual, and objective manner.Unlike essays or journal articles, a technical report focuses on presenting data, analysis, and results clearly so that others can understand, replicate, or make decisions based on the results.

• Purpose: Documentary and communicate technical information.

• To support decision-making or provide recommendations.

• To record experiments or design results for future reference.

• Demonstrate technical competence and professionalism.

• Written characteristics for a specific purpose and a specific audience.

• Structured into sections such as the Abstract, the Methodology, the Results and the Conclusions.

• Focuses on objectivity , clarity and precision .

• Incorporate visual data such as graphs, charts, tables and figures.

• Written in a formal and impersonal style, avoiding emotion or prejudice.

Common applications • Laboratory reports in universities.

• Project reports or engineering/business feasibility reports.

• Research reports for scientific documentation.

• Reports on the progress or state of the organizations.

TECHNICAL PAPER WRITING & IPR [B23AC3201 ] A.Neelima , Asst Prof, CSE CIC Sagi Rama Krishnam Raju Engineering College (A) 8 Voice in Technical Writing Voice refers to the relationship between subject and action (active vs passive).

Active Voice • The subject performs the action.

Example: The engineer tested the prototype. • Called, direct, but can sound personal.

Passive voice • The subject receives the action.

Example: The prototype was tested by the engineer. • More objective and commonly used in technical contexts.

When using • Use active voice when explaining who is doing the important thing.

• Use passive al centrarse on what happened, not who did it.

Balancing too much passive voice can make the writing ambiguous, alternating between active and passive to get clarity and emphasis.

The right format greatly improves readability, professionalism and consistency.

Essential format elements • Source: Times New Roman or Arial, 12 p.

• Line spacing: 1.5 or double.

• Margin: 1 inch in all directions.

• Numbered headings and subheadings.

• Figures and tables duly labeled and referenced in the text.

• Uniform citation style (APA, IEEE, etc.).

Components of the document • Title page: Number, title of the report, organization, date.

• Table of contents: automatically generated for navigation.

• Page count: Consistent everywhere.
